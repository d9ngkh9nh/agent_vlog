The 10 Pieces I Actually
Wear Every Single Week

Alex Nguyen 5 min read Style Diaries
Alex Nguyen
April 1, 2026 · 5 min read
Style Diaries Capsule Wardrobe Essentials Wardrobe Edit

Do you ever open your wardrobe and feel like you have nothing to wear, even though there's stuff everywhere? I've been there. For years, actually. It took a brutal wardrobe audit, a lot of donation bags, and one very honest mirror moment for me to finally figure out what I was actually reaching for — and what was just taking up space.

What I discovered after tracking my outfits for two full months straight (yes, I kept a spreadsheet — no shame) was that the same 10 pieces kept showing up, week after week, in different combinations. Everything else was essentially furniture.

These aren't the most glamorous pieces I own. They're not even the most expensive. But they're the ones that make me feel like myself every time I put them on — and that, I think, is the whole point.

"Style isn't about having more. It's about having the perfect version of exactly what you actually need."
— Alex Nguyen, Clozet
01 / The Foundation

The Perfect White Tee

Before you roll your eyes — hear me out. I've tried dozens of white tees. Boxy ones, fitted ones, oversized vintage ones from dusty thrift bins. The right one is genuinely life-changing. Mine is 100% heavy-weight cotton with a slightly relaxed fit and a crew neck that doesn't bag out after three washes.

I wear it tucked into trousers, knotted over a midi skirt, layered under an open blazer. It's the quiet workhorse of my wardrobe and the piece I'd repurchase without hesitation if it disappeared tomorrow.

A perfectly weighted white tee — the kind that goes with absolutely everything.

02 / The Workhorse

High-Waisted Straight-Leg Jeans

Not skinny. Not flared. Not wide-leg. Just a clean, straight cut that hits right at the ankle and sits high enough to elongate without being uncomfortable. A medium-to-dark wash is key — versatile enough for a coffee run and sharp enough for a dinner reservation.

The denim has structure — it holds its shape through a full day without going baggy at the knees. I could write an essay on the denim industry's relationship with stretch fabric, but I'll spare you. All I'll say is: try a pair with at most 2% elastane. You'll feel the difference.

Worn with the white tee and ankle boots, this is my default "I need to look like I tried without actually trying" combination. It hasn't failed me once.

03 / The Comfort Upgrade

A Cashmere (or Cashmere-Blend) Sweater

I used to think cashmere was a luxury I couldn't justify. Then I bought one good one — a neutral oatmeal crewneck — and I have never looked back. The secret is buying one neutral shade you'll reach for constantly rather than a fun colour that only works with half your wardrobe.

Mine goes over a collared shirt, tied loosely over my shoulders in summer, tucked into trousers when I need to look intentional. It washes beautifully on a wool cycle and somehow gets better with age. This is the piece that makes a good outfit feel elevated without any extra effort.

04 / The Statement Layer

A Tailored Blazer in Camel or Cream

If there's one piece that transitions seasons effortlessly, it's a well-cut blazer in a warm neutral. I wear mine open over a tee, belted over a slip dress, or draped over my shoulders when I want that effortless-French-girl thing that Instagram makes look very casual but is actually very calculated.

Key fit details: the shoulders should sit exactly right (not the most exciting advice, but the most honest one), the sleeves should fall just at the wrist, and there should be enough room in the chest to layer without it pulling.

05 / The Outerwear

A Trench Coat That Fits Like It Was Made for You

The trench coat: the only outerwear you'll ever truly need in a temperate climate.

I live in Hanoi. Our "winter" is aggressive humidity and the occasional chill that makes you feel dramatic for wearing a coat. And yet — I wear my trench constantly. It works over everything: jeans, dresses, pyjama trousers when I'm getting groceries at 8am and hoping nobody I know is there.

A classic khaki/camel trench is the move. Double-breasted, belted, knee-length. The proportions matter enormously. Try it before you buy it, and make sure you can move your arms without the whole thing lifting like a cape.

06 / The Easy Dress

A Silk (or Silk-Like) Slip Dress

The slip dress is having a permanent moment because it genuinely works for everything. Worn alone in summer. Layered over a turtleneck in winter. Dressed up with heels, dressed down with vintage sneakers and a hoodie layered underneath.

Mine is a bias-cut midi in a champagne-adjacent neutral. It moves beautifully and somehow photographs like I spent much more time getting dressed than I actually did. The goal.

07 — 10

The Final Four: Quiet but Essential

The last four pieces are quieter — they don't lead the outfit, but remove them and everything falls apart. Together they form the backbone of how I actually get dressed every morning.

07
Classic Ankle Boots

A pointed or almond toe in black or tan leather. The shoe that makes every single outfit look finished. Non-negotiable.

08
A Simple Leather Tote

Structured enough to hold its shape, large enough to hold your life. In tan, black, or a warm cognac. This is your daily bag.

09
A Striped Linen Shirt

Lightweight, breathable, works unbuttoned over a tee or tucked into trousers. The casual-smart crossover piece you'll wear to death.

10
Black Wide-Leg Trousers

Tailored enough for a meeting, relaxed enough for everything else. A high waist, a clean drape, and a hem that just skims the floor.

If I'm being honest, the hardest part of building a wardrobe that actually works isn't finding the right pieces — it's letting go of the wrong ones. Every good outfit I wear now started with a clear-out, not a shopping trip.

These 10 pieces aren't magic. They just happen to fit well, work together, and make me feel like I have my life together even when I absolutely do not. And honestly? That's everything I need from a wardrobe.
